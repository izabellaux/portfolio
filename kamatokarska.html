I like to cooperate, that is why (if possible) I always try to organize brainstorming sessions with clients (here, Kama). This helps them to know what they want, and it helps me to understand them better. Brainstorming is a great opportunity to ask the client more detailed questions. During these kind of workshops the environment is nice, and people think outside the box.

After the brainstorming and research, I know what content can I use on the website. What is done, and what has to be done in the future.

Even if I know from the beginning, why clients want me to design for them, I always try to focus more carefully on this aspect. In this case Kama's goals are following (see the photo on the right). At this stage, I knew what we have and what are our drivers. It was time to identify potential viewers (future clients). The grouped these ideas into potential personas (see the photo on the left).

By the same token, potential clients are identified at this point. Taking the fact, that because of lack of budget I could not conduct professional interviews. That is why I interviewed people from a close environment asking them about helpful things. What is more, I studied online wedding forums to investigate wedding couples needs and expectations. On the basis of gathered materials I developed following personas:

PreviousNextClose

Because of the limited time, I needed to get the most I could. On the basis of the interviews, I summarized how people look for an event host (MC). I presented it in the form of user thoughts, going around in people heads.

1: "Ok, I need to find the best event host (MC) for an upcoming event."
2: "I will ask my friends, whether they know someone great."
3: "Ehh, what a pity. They don't. I need to find him/her by myself."
4: "I need to take my laptop and open Google search."
5: "I will start from EVENT HOST (MC)."
6: "I will focus on the first site of Google's results"

Ok, now its time to know what they are looking for when searching for an event host?

After the interviews, it is clear that people want to see at first, the face of the potential event host (MC), rather than the experience:

It can be also stated that people the most want to see the potential event host in action, so the movie is a must-have. However, they admit that if they would be in a place that they cannot listen to the movie, they would read the textual description. Then, watch all the movies at home, or other comfortable environment.

What is more, Kama wanted to know, whether references on the website are important. A little more than half of the people wanted to see them (see the graph below). However, they also stated that their personal impression is the most important factor.

Let's get back to our user's thoughts. Our user enters the website.

1: "Ok, I need to find a photo of this person."
2: "Nice, maybe I can find some movies from the event hosting, etc."
3: "Let me also check his/her Social Media accounts. Is he/she popular?"
4: "I really like this person, know is time for the offer. How can I contact him/her?"
5: "Ok, the mail is sent. It's time to wait for an answer."

W I R E F R A M E S

Now I have to admit because I am a fan of precision and analysis, I scheduled a meeting with an SEO specialist. Even if I know the basic rules of search engine optimization, I wanted to discuss some detailed things. As I agreed on interviews, people will basically search for MC on the Internet, the SEO needs to be faultless.

I really like to sometimes take a break from current stuff and look at sth different (like colours, to get some fresh point of you). That is why, after 1h 'break' consumed on choosing photos etc, I rechecked the concept and added some notes.

The website final look was designed basing on the research, all the agreements and chosen style.
